Cyber Safety Buddy

Learn how to stay safe on the internet

The internet is useful for learning, games, videos, messages, and work, but it also has scams, fake links, weak passwords, and phishing tricks. This site helps you learn the basics in a simple way.

Why internet safety matters

Internet safety means protecting your accounts, private information, photos, messages, money, and devices. A scammer can use fake websites, urgent messages, or stolen passwords to get into your accounts.

A strong password is one of your best protections. A weak password like 123456789 is easy to guess. A strong password is longer, uses different types of characters, and is not predictable.

Remember

Big Safety Tips

Use strong passwords

Make passwords long. Mix uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Do not use your name, birthday, or 123456789.

Check links before clicking

Scammers often use links that look close to real websites. If a link looks strange, do not click it.

Do not share private info

Never send passwords, card numbers, phone numbers, or login codes into random forms, pop-ups, or messages.

Use two-factor login

Two-factor authentication adds a second step when logging in. It can protect you even if someone learns your password. Use an authenticator app or phone code whenever an important account offers it.
